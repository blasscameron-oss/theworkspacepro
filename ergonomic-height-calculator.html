How These Measurements Work

These calculations are based on standard ergonomic formulas used by occupational health professionals and the OSHA computer workstation guidelines.

  • Standing desk height (keyboard surface) = ~55% of your standing height — elbows at 90° while standing
  • Sitting desk height = ~27% of your standing height — derived from seated elbow height
  • Chair seat height = ~25% of your standing height — feet flat on floor, thighs parallel
  • Monitor height = top of screen at or just below eye level (~93% of height)
  • Monitor distance = one arm's length (adjusted proportionally)
  • Keyboard height = elbows at 90°, wrists straight — matches sitting desk surface
Note: These are starting guidelines. Everyone's body proportions are different. Use these as a baseline and adjust based on comfort. The most important rule: your joints should be at approximately 90° angles — elbows, hips, knees, and ankles.
