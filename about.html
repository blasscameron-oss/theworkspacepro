About

We do the research so you don't have to.

The Workspace Pro is an independent resource for ergonomic workspace recommendations. No fake experts, no fluff — just well-researched guidance.

Our Approach

There are hundreds of "best office chair" lists on the internet. Most are written by people who never sat in the chairs. We do things differently.

📐

Spec-Driven

We compare actual product specifications — seat pan dimensions, weight capacity, height range, adjustability points — not marketing copy.

🔬

Standards-Based

Our ergonomic recommendations are grounded in established standards from OSHA, BIFMA, and peer-reviewed ergonomic research.

🤝

Independent

We don't take payment from manufacturers for positive reviews. Affiliate commissions don't influence our recommendations.

How We Make Money

This site is free. We participate in affiliate programs with Amazon and select retailers. When you buy through our links, we may earn a commission at no extra cost to you. This funds our research and keeps the site running.

We are not paid by any manufacturer to recommend their products. If a product is bad, we say so — or we don't include it at all. Our reputation is worth more than any commission check.

A note on honesty: We don't claim to have a team of physical therapists on staff. We're researchers who aggregate and synthesize ergonomic standards, product specifications, and real user feedback into actionable guidance. If you have chronic pain, please consult a healthcare professional.

What We Don't Do

  • We don't accept paid placements or sponsored reviews (we will clearly label any sponsored content if that ever changes)
  • We don't fabricate testing data or user testimonials
  • We don't claim expertise we don't have
  • We don't recommend products we wouldn't use ourselves

Standards we reference

When we talk about neutral posture, monitor height, or reach zones, we lean on publicly available ergonomic guidance such as OSHA computer workstation guidance and furniture industry resources from BIFMA. Product recommendations still start from published specs and fit-for-purpose reasoning — not paid rankings or fabricated “lab tests.”
