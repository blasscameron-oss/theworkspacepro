A tall desk is not automatically a good desk for a tall user. At its upper range, the frame must reach your keyboard target while remaining stable enough for typing and supporting a monitor at a separate eye-level target.

## Start with two measurements

Measure your comfortable keyboard surface in both seated and standing positions. Keep your upper arms near your sides, shoulders relaxed, and wrists close to straight. Use the [height calculator](/ergonomic-height-calculator) as an estimate, then confirm with your own body and footwear.

The desk must cover both measurements after the desktop and any casters are included.

## Maximum height needs context

Compare the published maximum with these questions:

- Does the range include the desktop or only the frame?
- Is maximum height measured with leveling feet or optional casters?
- Does the manufacturer publish stability information or frame geometry?
- Will a monitor arm place additional leverage near the back edge?
- Is the control box or crossbar reducing usable knee clearance while seated?

Two desks can share the same maximum height and feel different because leg spacing, column stages, desktop mass, and floor level affect movement.

## Desktop depth matters more for tall users

NIOSH describes an external monitor placed roughly an arm's length away with the top at or below eye level. A deeper top gives you more room to separate monitor distance from keyboard reach. With a shallow desktop, a monitor arm may help move the display backward, but the clamp and wall clearance must fit.

## Tall-user desk checklist

| Check | Why it matters |
| --- | --- |
| Loaded maximum height | Confirms the keyboard can reach your standing elbow position |
| Stability near your target | Wobble often becomes more noticeable as columns extend |
| Desktop depth | Preserves screen distance without pushing the keyboard away |
| Monitor-arm capacity | Supports screen height independently from desk height |
| Cable slack | Allows full travel without pulling plugs or hubs |
| Seated minimum | Ensures the same desk also works when you sit |

## Avoid compensating with posture

If the surface is too low, do not solve it by rounding your back or leaning on locked elbows. If the monitor is low, raise the display independently instead of raising the keyboard above a comfortable position.

Choose based on your measured working range and the complete configuration—not the tallest number in a comparison table.
