Seat depth is the usable distance from the backrest to the front edge of the seat. It matters because a seat can support your thighs without pressing into the backs of your knees—but only when its depth matches your body.

## The quick fit check

Sit against the backrest with your feet supported. Leave a small gap between the front of the seat and the back of your lower leg so the edge does not press into you. Treat that as an adjustment check, not a universal measurement: body proportions vary even among people of the same height.

If the front edge presses into your legs, the seat is too deep or the backrest is not supporting you in the position you actually use. If a large part of your thighs is unsupported, the seat may be too shallow.

## Measure your body before comparing chairs

1. Sit on a firm surface with your back supported.
2. Keep your knees comfortably bent and your feet supported.
3. Measure horizontally from your back to the crease behind your knee.
4. Subtract enough room to avoid contact behind the knee.
5. Compare that usable target with the manufacturer's published seat-depth range.

Do not substitute overall chair depth for seat depth. Overall depth often includes the backrest, frame, or casters and tells you more about floor space than body fit.

## Fixed versus adjustable seat depth

An adjustable sliding seat is valuable when several people share a chair or when your measurement falls near the edge of a fixed seat's stated depth. Check whether the published range describes usable seat depth, adjustment travel, or the chair's full footprint.

| Situation | What to prioritize |
| --- | --- |
| Shorter thighs | A shallow minimum depth and a backrest you can reach |
| Longer thighs | Adequate maximum depth without losing knee clearance |
| Shared chair | A clear seat-slider range and simple controls |
| Feet do not reach the floor | Correct chair height first, then add a stable footrest if needed |

## Seat depth is only one part of fit

A chair can have the right depth and still fail if its minimum height is too tall, its armrests block you from approaching the desk, or its lumbar support does not meet your back. OSHA's neutral-position guidance also emphasizes supported feet, relaxed shoulders, straight wrists, and a supported back.

Use seat depth as an early filter. Then verify seat height, armrest clearance, backrest adjustment, return policy, and warranty before purchasing.

## When discomfort persists

Stop treating the chair as the only possible cause. Desk height, keyboard reach, long static periods, and existing health conditions may all contribute. Persistent numbness, weakness, or significant pain warrants assessment by a qualified health professional.
