For a shorter user, a standing desk's most important specification is often its **minimum loaded height**. Many desks descend low enough for standing work but remain too high for comfortable seated keyboard use.

## Estimate, then measure

Use the [ergonomic height calculator](/ergonomic-height-calculator) for a starting range. Next, recreate that height at your current desk with a tape measure. Sit with your feet supported, shoulders relaxed, elbows near your sides, and wrists straight. Measure from the floor to the surface supporting your keyboard.

That real measurement is more useful than height alone because arm, torso, and leg proportions vary.

## Compare the correct specification

Manufacturers may publish frame height without the desktop. Add the actual top thickness unless the specification explicitly includes it. Casters can raise the entire range as well.

Before buying, verify:

- Minimum height with the selected desktop installed.
- Whether casters or a keyboard tray change the working height.
- Crossbar or frame clearance for your knees.
- The return policy if the desk cannot reach your measured position.
- Controller placement and whether it conflicts with drawers or armrests.

## When the desk is still too tall

Raising the chair to meet the desk can work only if your feet remain supported. OSHA notes that a footrest can support the feet when the work surface is not adjustable. Use a stable platform large enough to change position; avoid a small object that encourages you to perch or tuck your legs.

A keyboard tray can lower the input surface, but it also changes knee clearance and mouse space. Include its thickness and travel in your measurements.

## Short-user desk checklist

| Check | Pass condition |
| --- | --- |
| Seated keyboard surface | Reaches your measured neutral-elbow range |
| Foot support | Feet reach the floor or a stable footrest |
| Knee clearance | Frame, tray, and controls do not restrict movement |
| Standing range | Reaches your measured standing-elbow range |
| Monitor independence | Screen can reach eye level without forcing keyboard height |

## Do not optimize for a single frozen posture

A correct height is a starting point, not a position to hold all day. Alternate positions and take regular movement breaks. If a desk fits only when your shoulders lift, wrists bend, or feet lose support, its minimum range is not appropriate for you.
