Why Setup Matters

Your desk quietly shapes how you feel at work

Long desk days add up. Chair height, monitor distance, and lighting aren’t fashion choices — they affect posture, focus, and whether you end the day sore. We help you match gear to your body and budget before you buy the wrong thing twice.

🦴

Posture & back comfort

Seat height, lumbar support, and desk height work together. Get one wrong and the others fight you all day. Our guides break down what to adjust first.

👁️

Eyes & neck strain

Monitor too high, too close, or poorly lit forces you to crane forward. Small placement and lighting fixes often beat expensive “ergonomic” gimmicks.

🎯

Buy once, if you can

Most regret comes from guessing. The free assessment and calculators narrow options by budget, height, and work style so you’re shopping with a plan.

How We Work

Straight talk — no fake reviews

We don’t invent user quotes or pretend to run a product lab. Recommendations are built from specs, ergonomic standards, and public retailer data — with affiliate links disclosed.

Spec-first

Height ranges, adjustability, weight capacity, and materials — not marketing blurbs alone.

Standards-aware

Guidance aligned with common ergonomic principles (neutral posture, monitor height, reach zones).

Affiliate transparent

We may earn a commission if you buy through Amazon and other links. Same price for you. See our disclosure.

Independent

No pay-to-play rankings. If we wouldn’t recommend something for a given budget or body type, it stays off the shortlist.

Sources & standards: Workspace guidance aligns with common ergonomic principles described by OSHA’s computer workstation eTool and industry seating/furniture guidance such as BIFMA. FAQ

Common questions

Is the assessment really free?

Yes. No account, no paywall. We earn money only if you later buy through optional affiliate links — same price for you.

Do you run a product testing lab?

No. We don’t pretend to. Recommendations use product specs, ergonomic standards, and public retailer data. We’re transparent about that on our About page.

Are Amazon links affiliate links?

Many are. They use our Amazon Associates tag so we may earn a commission. We only shortlist products we’d recommend for a given budget and use case. Full details on our affiliate disclosure.

Can I share my quiz results?

Yes. After you finish, use Share results for a link that reopens your plan (answers only — no personal data). Great for comparing with a partner or posting in a remote-work chat.

What should I do first if I’m in pain?

Start with height and posture basics (our height calculator + back-pain guide), then chair support. Chronic pain needs a clinician — we’re not medical advice.
